03 - THE STRATEGY

How to Trade
the Pattern

1

Wait for First Hour Close

Patience is critical. Avoid trading on partial hour data. Wait for the complete 60-minute candle to close before determining direction. This eliminates false signals from intra-hour volatility and gives you the cleanest read on initial momentum.

IF hour_1_close > hour_1_open ‚Üí BULLISH SIGNAL
IF hour_1_close < hour_1_open ‚Üí BEARISH SIGNAL
2

Align Position with Direction

Once the first hour closes, establish your position in the same direction as the candle. Green first hour ‚Üí get long. Red first hour ‚Üí get short or stay flat. The 75% continuation rate gives you a statistical edge. Size positions accordingly.

3

Set Risk Parameters

Use the first hour range to define risk. Place stops just beyond the first hour's high (if short) or low (if long). This protects you if the pattern breaks. Target the day's close for exits. The edge exists intraday, holding overnight introduces different dynamics.

LONG: Stop = hour_1_low - (ATR * 0.5)
SHORT: Stop = hour_1_high + (ATR * 0.5)
EXIT: End of day session
4

Scale Position Size with Conviction

Not all first hours are equal. Larger range moves with strong volume deserve larger positions. Weak, indecisive first hours warrant reduced size. Consider the magnitude of the move, not just direction. A +5% first hour is a stronger signal than +0.5%.

If the day opens with a significant gap from the prior close, the first hour becomes less predictive. Gaps often fill, creating different dynamics that can override the momentum signal.
