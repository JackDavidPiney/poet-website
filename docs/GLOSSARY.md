# Glossary

## Overview
Comprehensive glossary of terms used in Poet, covering trading concepts, statistical methods, technical terminology, and project-specific vocabulary.

---

## Trading Terms

### Alpha
**Definition:** Excess return of a strategy relative to a benchmark.

**Example:** If SPY returns 10% and your strategy returns 15%, alpha = 5%.

**Related:** Beta, Sharpe Ratio

---

### Arbitrage
**Definition:** Simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset to profit from price differences.

**Example:** Buy SOL on Exchange A at $100, sell on Exchange B at $101.

**Related:** Market Inefficiency

---

### Ask (Offer)
**Definition:** Lowest price a seller is willing to accept.

**Example:** Ask = $100.50 means someone will sell at that price.

**Related:** Bid, Spread

---

### Backtest
**Definition:** Testing a strategy on historical data to evaluate performance.

**Example:** Applying the First Hour Effect to 2023 SOL data.

**Related:** Out-of-Sample, Walk-Forward, Overfitting

---

### Bear Market
**Definition:** Sustained period of declining prices (typically -20% or more).

**Example:** Crypto winter 2022 (-70% from peak).

**Related:** Bull Market, Correction

---

### Beta
**Definition:** Measure of an asset's volatility relative to the market.

**Example:** Beta = 1.5 means asset moves 1.5x the market's movements.

**Related:** Alpha, Correlation

---

### Bid
**Definition:** Highest price a buyer is willing to pay.

**Example:** Bid = $99.50 means someone will buy at that price.

**Related:** Ask, Spread

---

### Bull Market
**Definition:** Sustained period of rising prices.

**Example:** 2020-2021 crypto bull run (+300% for BTC).

**Related:** Bear Market, Rally

---

### Breakout
**Definition:** Price moving above resistance or below support.

**Example:** SOL breaks above $110 resistance, signaling potential uptrend.

**Related:** Support, Resistance, Range

---

### Candlestick
**Definition:** Chart representation showing open, high, low, close prices.

**Example:** Green candle = close > open (bullish).

**Related:** OHLC, Timeframe

---

### Cost Basis
**Definition:** Original value of an asset for tax purposes.

**Example:** Buy SOL at $100, cost basis = $100.

**Related:** Realized Gain, Unrealized Gain

---

### Derivative
**Definition:** Financial instrument whose value derives from an underlying asset.

**Example:** SOL futures, ETH options.

**Related:** Futures, Options, Perpetual

---

### Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
**Definition:** Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals.

**Example:** Buy $100 of BTC every Monday regardless of price.

**Related:** Lump Sum, Timing

---

### Entry Price
**Definition:** Price at which a position is opened.

**Example:** Long SOL at entry = $100.

**Related:** Exit Price, Cost Basis

---

### Exit Price
**Definition:** Price at which a position is closed.

**Example:** Exit SOL at $105 → 5% profit.

**Related:** Entry Price, P&L

---

### Fill
**Definition:** Execution of an order.

**Example:** "Order filled at $100.25" means trade executed at that price.

**Related:** Partial Fill, Slippage

---

### First Hour Effect
**Definition:** Poet strategy exploiting momentum in the first hour after market open.

**Example:** SOL breaks first hour high → enter long.

**Related:** Momentum, Intraday

---

### Frontrunning
**Definition:** Trading ahead of a large order to profit from anticipated price movement.

**Example:** Seeing 10,000 BTC buy order coming, buying first.

**Related:** Market Manipulation (often illegal)

---

### Futures
**Definition:** Contract to buy/sell an asset at a future date at a predetermined price.

**Example:** SOL March 2024 futures at $100.

**Related:** Perpetual, Spot

---

### Hedge
**Definition:** Position taken to offset potential losses.

**Example:** Long SOL spot, short SOL futures to hedge.

**Related:** Risk Management

---

### Leverage
**Definition:** Using borrowed capital to amplify position size.

**Example:** 5x leverage: $1,000 controls $5,000 worth of SOL.

**Related:** Margin, Liquidation

---

### Limit Order
**Definition:** Order to buy/sell at a specific price or better.

**Example:** Buy SOL at $100 (executes at $100 or lower).

**Related:** Market Order, Stop Order

---

### Liquidation
**Definition:** Forced closure of a leveraged position due to insufficient margin.

**Example:** Long SOL 10x at $100, liquidates at $90.

**Related:** Leverage, Margin Call

---

### Long
**Definition:** Buying an asset expecting price to rise.

**Example:** Long SOL at $100, sell at $110 → 10% profit.

**Related:** Short, Position

---

### Maker
**Definition:** Order that adds liquidity to the order book.

**Example:** Limit order placed and not immediately filled.

**Related:** Taker, Fees

---

### Market Order
**Definition:** Order to buy/sell immediately at current market price.

**Example:** "Buy SOL market" executes at best available ask price.

**Related:** Limit Order, Slippage

---

### Momentum
**Definition:** Tendency for price to continue in the same direction.

**Example:** SOL up 5% in an hour, likely to continue rising.

**Related:** Trend, Mean Reversion

---

### Net P&L (Profit & Loss)
**Definition:** Total gain or loss after all costs.

**Example:** Gross profit $100, fees $5 → Net P&L = $95.

**Related:** Gross P&L, Fees

---

### Order Book
**Definition:** List of buy and sell orders for an asset.

**Example:** Bids at $99, $98, $97; Asks at $100, $101, $102.

**Related:** Depth, Liquidity

---

### Perpetual (Perp)
**Definition:** Futures contract with no expiration date.

**Example:** SOL-PERP (never expires, unlike March futures).

**Related:** Futures, Funding Rate

---

### Position
**Definition:** Current holding in an asset.

**Example:** Long 10 SOL at $100 = position of $1,000.

**Related:** Long, Short, Open Interest

---

### Range
**Definition:** Price movement between support and resistance.

**Example:** SOL trading between $95 (support) and $105 (resistance).

**Related:** Breakout, Consolidation

---

### Resistance
**Definition:** Price level where selling pressure exceeds buying.

**Example:** SOL repeatedly fails to break $110 → resistance.

**Related:** Support, Breakout

---

### Reversal
**Definition:** Change in price trend direction.

**Example:** Downtrend → Uptrend after hitting support.

**Related:** Trend, Momentum

---

### Risk-Free Rate
**Definition:** Return on a zero-risk investment (e.g., US Treasury bonds).

**Example:** 4% annual yield on T-bills.

**Related:** Sharpe Ratio, Excess Return

---

### Short
**Definition:** Selling an asset expecting price to fall.

**Example:** Short SOL at $100, buy back at $90 → 10% profit.

**Related:** Long, Position

---

### Slippage
**Definition:** Difference between expected and actual execution price.

**Example:** Expected fill at $100, actual fill at $100.50 → 0.5% slippage.

**Related:** Market Order, Liquidity

---

### Spread
**Definition:** Difference between bid and ask prices.

**Example:** Bid $99.50, Ask $100.50 → Spread = $1.00.

**Related:** Liquidity, Trading Costs

---

### Stop Loss
**Definition:** Order to exit a position at a predetermined loss level.

**Example:** Long SOL at $100, stop loss at $95 → max loss 5%.

**Related:** Risk Management, Take Profit

---

### Support
**Definition:** Price level where buying pressure exceeds selling.

**Example:** SOL bounces at $95 multiple times → support.

**Related:** Resistance, Range

---

### Take Profit
**Definition:** Order to exit a position at a predetermined profit level.

**Example:** Long SOL at $100, take profit at $110 → lock in 10%.

**Related:** Stop Loss, Risk/Reward

---

### Taker
**Definition:** Order that removes liquidity from the order book.

**Example:** Market order or limit order that matches immediately.

**Related:** Maker, Fees

---

### Timeframe
**Definition:** Duration of each candlestick in a chart.

**Example:** 1h chart = each candle represents 1 hour.

**Related:** Candlestick, Chart

---

### Trend
**Definition:** General direction of price movement.

**Example:** Uptrend = series of higher highs and higher lows.

**Related:** Momentum, Reversal

---

### Volatility
**Definition:** Measure of price fluctuation.

**Example:** BTC moves ±5% daily = high volatility.

**Related:** Standard Deviation, VIX

---

## Statistical Terms

### Average (Mean)
**Definition:** Sum of values divided by count.

**Formula:** `(x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) / n`

**Example:** Returns [2%, 4%, 6%] → Mean = 4%

**Related:** Median, Mode

---

### Backtesting
**Definition:** Testing strategy on historical data.

**Example:** Apply First Hour Effect to 2023 data.

**Related:** Walk-Forward, Out-of-Sample

---

### Calmar Ratio
**Definition:** Return per unit of maximum drawdown.

**Formula:** `Annual Return / |Max Drawdown|`

**Example:** 50% return, -10% max DD → Calmar = 5.0

**Related:** Sharpe, Sortino, Max Drawdown

---

### Confidence Interval
**Definition:** Range likely to contain the true value with specified probability.

**Example:** Sharpe 2.0 with 95% CI [1.5, 2.5]

**Related:** Statistical Significance, P-Value

---

### Correlation
**Definition:** Measure of relationship between two variables (-1 to +1).

**Example:** SOL and BTC correlation = 0.85 (highly correlated)

**Related:** Covariance, Beta

---

### Cumulative Return
**Definition:** Total return over time, compounded.

**Formula:** `(Final Value / Initial Value - 1) × 100%`

**Example:** $100 → $125 = 25% cumulative return

**Related:** Annualized Return, CAGR

---

### Drawdown
**Definition:** Peak-to-trough decline in equity.

**Example:** Equity $110 → $95 = -13.6% drawdown

**Related:** Maximum Drawdown, Underwater Period

---

### Equity Curve
**Definition:** Chart showing account value over time.

**Example:** Start $100 → End $150, equity curve shows path.

**Related:** Cumulative Return, Drawdown

---

### Expected Value (EV)
**Definition:** Weighted average of all possible outcomes.

**Formula:** `Σ(Probability × Payoff)`

**Example:** 60% chance +$10, 40% chance -$5 → EV = $4

**Related:** Risk/Reward, Kelly Criterion

---

### Kurtosis
**Definition:** Measure of distribution tail heaviness.

**Example:** High kurtosis = fat tails (outliers more likely)

**Related:** Skewness, Normal Distribution

---

### Maximum Drawdown
**Definition:** Largest peak-to-trough decline.

**Example:** Equity $110 → $90 = -18.2% max drawdown

**Related:** Drawdown, Calmar Ratio

---

### Median
**Definition:** Middle value when data sorted.

**Example:** [1%, 3%, 5%, 7%, 9%] → Median = 5%

**Related:** Average, Quartiles

---

### Monte Carlo Simulation
**Definition:** Running many random simulations to model outcomes.

**Example:** Shuffle 100 trades 1,000 times, analyze distribution.

**Related:** Backtesting, Probability

---

### Normal Distribution
**Definition:** Bell curve; symmetric probability distribution.

**Example:** Many natural phenomena follow normal distribution.

**Related:** Standard Deviation, Z-Score

---

### Outlier
**Definition:** Data point significantly different from others.

**Example:** Returns [1%, 2%, 3%, 50%] → 50% is outlier

**Related:** Standard Deviation, Robust Statistics

---

### Out-of-Sample
**Definition:** Data not used in strategy development.

**Example:** Train on 2023, test on 2024 (out-of-sample).

**Related:** In-Sample, Walk-Forward

---

### Overfitting
**Definition:** Strategy optimized too closely to historical data.

**Example:** 20 parameters, perfect backtest, fails live → overfit.

**Related:** Curve Fitting, Out-of-Sample

---

### P-Value
**Definition:** Probability of observing results if null hypothesis true.

**Example:** p = 0.01 → 1% chance results due to randomness.

**Related:** Statistical Significance, Hypothesis Test

---

### Percentile
**Definition:** Value below which a percentage of data falls.

**Example:** 95th percentile = 95% of data below this value.

**Related:** Quartile, Median

---

### Profit Factor
**Definition:** Ratio of gross profits to gross losses.

**Formula:** `Σ(Wins) / Σ(|Losses|)`

**Example:** $300 profit, $100 loss → PF = 3.0

**Related:** Win Rate, Risk/Reward

---

### R-Squared (R²)
**Definition:** Proportion of variance explained by model (0 to 1).

**Example:** R² = 0.85 → Model explains 85% of variance.

**Related:** Regression, Correlation

---

### Risk-Adjusted Return
**Definition:** Return relative to risk taken.

**Example:** Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio

**Related:** Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar

---

### Sharpe Ratio
**Definition:** Return per unit of volatility.

**Formula:** `(Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation`

**Example:** 12% return, 4% risk-free, 8% std dev → Sharpe = 1.0

**Related:** Sortino, Calmar, Volatility

---

### Skewness
**Definition:** Measure of distribution asymmetry.

**Example:** Positive skew = more small losses, few large gains.

**Related:** Kurtosis, Distribution

---

### Sortino Ratio
**Definition:** Return per unit of downside volatility.

**Formula:** `(Return - Target) / Downside Deviation`

**Example:** Only penalizes downside volatility, not upside.

**Related:** Sharpe, Downside Risk

---

### Standard Deviation (σ)
**Definition:** Measure of dispersion around the mean.

**Example:** Returns [2%, 4%, 6%] → σ ≈ 2%

**Related:** Variance, Volatility

---

### Statistical Significance
**Definition:** Result unlikely due to chance alone.

**Example:** p < 0.05 → Statistically significant at 95% confidence.

**Related:** P-Value, Confidence Interval

---

### T-Statistic (T-Test)
**Definition:** Ratio of deviation to standard error.

**Example:** Test if mean return significantly different from zero.

**Related:** P-Value, Hypothesis Test

---

### Variance
**Definition:** Average squared deviation from mean.

**Formula:** `σ² = Σ(x - μ)² / n`

**Example:** Variance = (Std Deviation)²

**Related:** Standard Deviation, Volatility

---

### Win Rate
**Definition:** Percentage of profitable trades.

**Formula:** `(Winning Trades / Total Trades) × 100%`

**Example:** 65 wins, 35 losses → Win Rate = 65%

**Related:** Profit Factor, W/L Ratio

---

### Z-Score
**Definition:** Number of standard deviations from the mean.

**Formula:** `z = (x - μ) / σ`

**Example:** z = 2 → Value is 2 std devs above mean.

**Related:** Standard Deviation, Normal Distribution

---

## Technical Terms

### API (Application Programming Interface)
**Definition:** Set of rules for software communication.

**Example:** Poet API for fetching trade data.

**Related:** Endpoint, REST

---

### Async (Asynchronous)
**Definition:** Operations that don't block execution.

**Example:** `async function getData()` in JavaScript.

**Related:** Promise, Callback

---

### Backend
**Definition:** Server-side code and database.

**Example:** Python FastAPI server, PostgreSQL database.

**Related:** Frontend, Full Stack

---

### Base64
**Definition:** Encoding binary data as ASCII text.

**Example:** Encode image as base64 string for transmission.

**Related:** Encoding, Binary

---

### CDN (Content Delivery Network)
**Definition:** Distributed network of servers for faster content delivery.

**Example:** Google Fonts via CDN.

**Related:** Cache, Performance

---

### CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing)
**Definition:** Security mechanism for cross-domain requests.

**Example:** Frontend at poet.app accessing api.poet.app.

**Related:** Security, HTTP Headers

---

### CSS (Cascading Style Sheets)
**Definition:** Language for styling HTML.

**Example:** `color: #00ff88;` sets text to neon green.

---

### Deployment
**Definition:** Publishing code to production.

**Example:** Push to GitHub → Vercel auto-deploys.

**Related:** CI/CD, Production

---

### DevTools
**Definition:** Browser developer tools for debugging.

### DOM (Document Object Model)

### Endpoint

### Frontend
**Definition:** Client-side code users interact with.

### Git
**Definition:** Version control system.

**Example:** `git commit -m "Add feature"` saves changes.

### HTML (HyperText Markup Language)

**Example:** `<h1>Title</h1>` creates heading.

**Definition:** Performance report generated by Poet.

**Example:** Upload trades → Generate PDF factsheet.

**Related:** Metrics, PDF

---

### Poet
**Definition:** Platform for systematic and discretionary trading strategies.

**Tagline:** "Success driven by data"

**Related:** Quantitative Trading

---

### Skill
**Definition:** Folder with best practices for Claude (internal).

**Example:** docx skill for creating Word documents.

**Related:** Documentation (internal use)

---

### Strategy Page
**Definition:** Dedicated page for each trading strategy.

**Example:** `first_hour_strategy.html`
